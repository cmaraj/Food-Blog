Pepper Sauce

Pepper Sauce

An inviting way to add some heat to your meal is it add some pepper sauce on the side. Feel free to add it on top of your dish and mix it in or keep it on the side and touch your food with it. Either way you will be adding a lot of flavor and power to your spicy chicken recipes. An amazing brand is Matouk’s, which adds heat without ruining the flavor.

Simple Spices

Don't be shy, add as much spice as you can handle. Each spicy chicken recipe is different, and can be complimented in a number of ways with the spices you choose to add. Paprika, Cayenne, chili (flakes, oil, powder), pepper, are all wonderful sources of flavor and heat!

Whole Peppers

One of the simplest ways to pack on the heat in your dish is with the addition of hot peppers. Scotch bonnet, habanero, jalapenos, serrano, etc. are all fantastic peppers that can add unique and powerful spice to your dish. Throw the peppers in whole, diced, or pitted depending on your spice tolerance. All of these options will take your spicy chicken recipes to another level.

Atlanta's Spiciest Chicken Sauce

If you haven’t already heard, Atlanta’s very own restaurant Joella’s Hot Chicken has released one of the countries spiciest chicken filled menus! Joella’s Hot Chicken was established in Louisville, Kentucky back in 2015. They offer chicken in many different ways: Giant chicken tenders, huge chicken wings, and chicken and waffles. “The best part of ordering has to be picking your sauce. There are six that you can choose from and they get hotter as you go up the list.” Customers can also choose their heat level from no heat, sweet or mild, all the way to the need to sign a waiver to eat the dish. Their prize sauce is called “Fire-In-Da-Hole” and is so hot that customers literally have to sign a waiver to even eat it! The main source of the sauce heat comes from one of the spiciest peppers in the world, the Carolina Reaper! The Carolina Reaper has a Scoville Heat Unit of 2 million! To put that in perspective, a typical jalapeno pepper is between 2500 – 8000 Scoville Heat Units. If you are looking to test your ability to handle some serious heat Joella’s Hot Chicken is exactly the place to do it, just don’t forget to sign their waiver first.
